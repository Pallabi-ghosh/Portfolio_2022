Konsus ApS

Reduce your electricity consumption and your electricity bill, and help the environment.

Overview

In my second internship, I worked as an UI/UX Designer and Frontend Developer at Konsus which was a sustainability startup in Copenhagen. I designed the wireframe, prototyped in Figma. Also, I coded the mobile app from scratch in react native. It was designed for the customers who wants to reduce their electricity consumption. Not only it would reduce the electricity bill but also it would help to endorse a sustainable lifestyle.

Role & Responsibilities

  • User Design
  • Prototyping
  • Visual Design
  • Usability Test
  • React Native

My design process

Inspiration

I started the inspiration phase of my process by market research and competitor analysis to better understand who my competitors were and what they did well or poorly.

Problem Statement & Solution

My findings also helped me shape my problem statement and possible solutions. My original problem statement has remained consistent throughout the app’s development, though the solutions have changed slightly as I’ve progressed.

User Interviews

I conducted remote user interviews to gain a well-rounded understanding of my users’ needs, goals, and points of friction. I asked questions focused on users' experiences with home renovation. I then analyzed my users' answers by sorting them into an affinity map to gain overarching insights. The user research helped me confirm or revise my possible solutions and user stories.

Wireframes

Next, I started sketching low-fidelity wireframes with pen and paper. I moved on to Figma for my mid- and high-fidelity wireframes. Throughout the different fidelity stages, continuous iterations were made to improve the app’s functionality and UX.

Style guide

In our shared Figma file, we compiled all of our components, colors, and typefaces. This later helped us to build a style guide to show and instruct others on our final choices for all user interface aspects.

For the typography, I have used Josefin Sans. For all other text, I used Montserrat font, to ensure legibility.

Final Reflections

  • Though, I was more drawn to creating wireframes and prototypes, I found that the important part of the process was testing. For example, usability testing was crucial in finding errors and seeing things from a different perspective.
  • Based on feedback from my usability testing, I also conducted a preference test to see which version of the home screen participants preferred.
  • Collecting constructive criticism was another important part of the process.
  • I would also like to increase the accessibility of the app, especially for older users.
